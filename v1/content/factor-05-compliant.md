# Factor 6: Compliant [draft]

**Definition:** Compliant data meets regulatory requirements with enforced access controls, clear ownership, and auditable AI-specific safeguards.

## Why It Matters

AI systems can't be used if they create legal, regulatory, or ethical liability. Compliance isn't optionalâ€”it's existential. GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and emerging AI regulations impose strict requirements on how data can be collected, stored, processed, and used for AI. Violations result in fines, lawsuits, and reputational damage.

## Requirements

- Explicit data ownership and stewardship
- Role-based access controls enforced at query time
- PII detection and masking for AI workloads
- Bias monitoring in training data
- Audit trails for data access and usage
- Policies for acceptable AI use cases per dataset
- Consent tracking for AI-specific data usage
- Data retention and deletion capabilities

## Key Questions

- Do you have explicit consent to use your data for AI/ML purposes?
- Can you demonstrate compliance to an auditor?
- How do you detect and mitigate bias in training data?
- Can you fulfill a "right to be forgotten" request across all AI systems?
